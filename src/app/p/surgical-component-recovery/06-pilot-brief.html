Surgical Component Recovery

A small pilot to test whether unused, inner-sealed heart and brain procedure-set components can be documented, priced, and routed to a licensed buyer without touching used devices or medical waste.

Verdict Worth a tiny pilot. Not a business yet.

The first move is not a shipment. It is a buyer legality sheet and one real set audit.

The source

U.S. heart and brain surgeons identify opened sets with unused sealed leftovers.

The buyer

India-side contacts test demand and the lawful import/distribution route.

The product

Only unused, untouched, inner-sealed, in-date components with label/lot/expiry data.

The stop line

No used, exposed, reprocessed, red-bag, or customs-ambiguous goods.

Why It Is Interesting

Real waste

OR waste is documented.

Hospitals discard meaningful unused operating-room supplies. REMEDY-style recovery and donation programs prove that sealed surplus recovery is a real category, not just a theory.

Contact edge

The contacts make it testable.

The U.S. surgeons can show which sets create leftovers. The buyer-side contacts can answer the only question that matters: can these exact categories be bought legally?

Pilot question: can one tiny batch be documented, accepted, and paid for legally?

Bright Lines

Allowed
  • Inner sterile seal intact
  • Never touched patient
  • In-date with lot/expiry/UDI
  • Transferred before waste stream
Not allowed
  • Used or patient-contact items
  • Broken seal or exposed sterile goods
  • Red-bag or regulated waste
  • Cleaning or re-sterilizing
Unresolved
  • Buyer import license for exact categories
  • "Not for individual sale" components
  • U.S. repackager/relabeler registration duties
  • Hospital title-transfer terms

Actual Steps

Buyer legality sheetName the Indian buying/importing entity. Confirm CDSCO MD-15 access and the residual shelf-life rule for the exact categories.
Target category listAsk what they want and what they would pay: cannulas, guidewires, hemostats, sutures, clips, patties, drains, graft-related components, and similar sealed items.
One U.S. set auditWith one surgeon, audit one heart or brain procedure set. Record manufacturer, REF/model, lot, expiry, UDI, intact seal, standalone label, and "not for individual sale" language.
Two narrow professional opinionsAsk U.S. counsel to confirm repackager/relabeler registration duties and that this avoids reprocessor/manufacturer status. Ask an India regulatory consultant if the buyer can receive these exact items as unused goods.
Micro-batch onlyIf green, run one site, one procedure type, one trained champion, one clean collection bin, one item-level ledger, and one pre-approved buyer list.

Success And Hard Stops

Promising if
  • Buyer can legally receive the exact categories
  • One set produces meaningful clean value
  • Items have standalone label, lot, expiry, and UDI
  • Hospital can transfer title before waste entry
  • Buyer gives SKU-level pricing before shipment
Stop if
  • Buyer cannot show a legal import/distribution path
  • Most items are short-dated, unlabeled, or ambiguous
  • Hospital requires items to enter waste first
  • Counsel says this triggers reprocessor/manufacturer obligations
  • Manufacturer anti-resale or gray-market terms block the SKU
  • Buyer asks for used, exposed, or reprocessed goods
